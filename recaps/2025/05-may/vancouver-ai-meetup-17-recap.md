## Technical Education: How AI Really Works

### Kush's Search Evolution & Open Access Advocacy

**Presentation Structure:** Two-part talk covering search history and current problems.

#### Part 1: Evolution of Search Technology

**Historical Progression:**
1. **Ancient Libraries (2000 BC):** Manual categorization and taxonomies (Library of Alexandria)
2. **Digital Era:** Exact string matching with early computers
3. **Error Tolerance:** Levenshtein distance for typo handling (1960s)
4. **Boolean Logic:** AND/OR/NOT operations for complex queries (1965-1975)
5. **Relevance Ranking:** TF-IDF and BM25 algorithms for importance weighting
6. **Semantic Search:** Embedding models enabling meaning-based matching

#### Part 2: Why Search Still Sucks

**Core Problems Identified:**

**1. Private Index Monopoly**
"Who allowed a private index to be the de facto index for the open web? What a great idea? Who the fuck did that?" - Google's control over web indexing despite internet being public infrastructure.

**2. Content Format Evolution** 
Most valuable information now in videos, images, and audio formats that resist traditional indexing, creating massive knowledge gaps.

**3. Platform Lockdowns**
- **Reddit API Closure:** $60-70M deals with OpenAI/Google locked out public access to community-contributed knowledge
- **Twitter API Pricing:** $40,000/month making access impossible for most users
- **Discord/Slack Migration:** Open source communities moving to non-indexable platforms

**4. Knowledge Gatekeeping**
"If you're not part of that given Discord, if you're not part of that given community on Slack or on WhatsApp, you simply cannot find that information."

#### Aaron Swartz Tribute & Call to Action

**Historical Context:** Aaron Swartz pursued for downloading academic papers from JSTOR (an open network), faced 35-year charges, died by suicide after 2-year legal persecution.

**Open Access Manifesto:** Kush highlighted Swartz's vision for universal access to human knowledge and information freedom.

**Personal Philosophy:** "My job is to say this is fucked, burn it down and then your guys' job is to find the solution... I don't need to have the solution."

**Community Action Required:** "Unless we actively participate in the communities that we build, being more open, the software that we build, having different values, et cetera, we will continue to go down this road."

## Critical Technology Analysis

### Search and Information Freedom Crisis

**Kush's Core Arguments:**
1. **Private Control of Public Infrastructure:** Google's monopoly over web indexing represents fundamental threat to information democracy
2. **Knowledge Platform Degradation:** Reddit, Twitter, Discord migrations creating inaccessible information silos  
3. **AI Training Data Capture:** Closed-source AI systems capturing public community contributions without compensation
4. **Search Quality Decline:** SEO manipulation and platform fragmentation destroying information discovery

**Historical Parallel:** Aaron Swartz's persecution for advocating open access to academic information, demonstrating ongoing tension between information freedom and institutional control.

**Community Response Required:** Active participation in building open alternatives rather than relying on existing commercial platforms.

### Information Access Philosophy

**The Free Service Trap:** "They gave us Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Drive, all these things for free. And we slurped that shit up, and then we end up with private company owning the index of the whole public internet."

**Anarchist Approach:** "My job is to say this is fucked, burn it down and then your guys' job is to find the solution."

**Practical Implications:** Community must actively choose open-source alternatives and contribute to open knowledge commons to prevent further information capture.
